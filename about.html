    About Citizen Advocacy

  • Citizen Advocacy is a registered charity founded in 1974, we harness the power of the people of Ottawa to provide hope and support to the city’s most vulnerable citizens.

  • What is the “Everyday Champions” program about?

  • Everyday Champions is a unique program that matches “advocates”, people of all walks of life in the community with “protégés”, those who are living with a disability and may be isolated and vulnerable with nowhere else to turn for friendship and support. Many of these matches become long standing mutually rewarding relationships that flourish over decades.

  • How many people is the Everyday Champions program serving?

  • Over the years, Citizen Advocacy has facilitated thousands of matches with those who live with mental and physical disabilities. Currently, there are 275 “matches”, 179 female and 96 male. They come from all walks of life and include all ages of adults.

  • Here are some vital statistics about Citizen Advocacy clients:

  • Physical disability: 79%
  • Psychiatric disability or mental health diagnosis: 36%
  • Developmental delay including autism and aspergers: 60%
  • Elderly (age related disability): 5%
  • Dual diagnosis and/or multiple disabilities (This may include a developmental disability and mental health diagnosis as well as other co-morbidities): 95%
